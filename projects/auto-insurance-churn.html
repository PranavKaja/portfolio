MSN-05 // DEPLOYED

AUTO INSURANCE CHURN

18% → 11% projected churn across 350K policyholders.

350K Records18% → 11% ChurnSMOTE PipelineClass Imbalance

BRIEFING

Random Forest and Gradient Boosting classifiers on 350,000 policyholder records to flag retention risk early. The hard part wasn't the model — it was the severe class imbalance (churners are rare). A SMOTE resampling pipeline rebalanced the training data so precision and recall held steady across high-risk segments instead of collapsing onto the majority class. The model points retention spend at the policyholders most likely to leave, projecting churn down from 18% to 11%.

ROLE / METHOD / OUTCOME

Role
Solo build.
Method
Random Forest + Gradient Boosting with a SMOTE resampling pipeline.
Outcome
Projected churn cut from 18% to 11%.

STACK: Python · scikit-learn · Gradient Boosting · SMOTE
